Introduction

Worldwide, around three billion people play fascinating video games regularly. They owe their immersion in an alternate reality as a means of escape from the routine and struggles of the daily grind to Game Testers. These gaming enthusiasts turn their passion for playing video games to professional use when they identify bugs, flaws and areas of improvement in games under development.

Also Known As:
  • Video Games Tester
  • Computer Games Tester

Typical Job Responsibilities

  • Thoroughly examine video games that are under development to ensure they are free of errors, bugs or glitches before the final version is released to the public

  • Create test plans, scenarios and procedures for new games

  • Test different versions of the game to find bugs or problems with the gameplay, graphics and audio

  • Figure out what is causing the bug and suggest improvements and solutions that will fix it

  • Test different playstyles after anticipating all the ways the game could be played

  • Check the playability and performance of the game and recommend improvements

  • Check for spelling mistakes and copyright issues (text or logos on different items in the game)

  • Report and document all bugs and problems in a quality management system

  • Assess the game against those already on the market

  • Collaborate with other testers to find the issues in multiplayer

  • Evaluate the accessibility options designed for people with disabilities

Standard Work Environment

Game Testers are office-based, and most of their work is done sitting at a desk using a computer. They work in teams consisting of other testers, developers, programmers, artists and designers. After the release of the game, they will collaborate with the customer support team too. More often than not, remote and overseas work are viable options.

Work Schedule

Typically Game Testers work full-time for around 35 hours/week. However, strict deadlines often demand extended work hours. As the game nears completion, you may have to work overtime and take early morning, evening and weekend shifts. Freelancing and self-employed Game Testers will have more flexible work hours.

Employers

Finding a new job might seem challenging. Game Testers can boost their job search by asking their network for referrals, contacting companies directly, accessing job search platforms and specialist game publications, leveraging social media and inquiring at staffing agencies. Jobs can also be obtained on a short-term contract or freelance basis.

Game Testers are generally employed by:
  • Specialist Outsourcing Game-Testing Companies
  • Game Publishers
  • Game Development Studios
Workplace Challenges
  • Repetitive work which needs a lot of attention to detail
  • Stress due to strict deadlines and long work hours when the game is approaching release
  • Health issues due to long hours spent sitting at a desk and staring at a computer screenHealth issues due to long hours spent sitting at a desk and staring at a computer screen

Suggested Work Experience

  • Apprenticeships provide prospective Game Testers with a structured entry route into the gaming industry. You will gain optimal benefit from your tasks outside of the classroom when they line up perfectly with your lessons inside it. You may get to hear countless stories from more experienced professionals and obtain valuable hands-on experience when they manage to turn seemingly routine incidents into unique learning moments.
  • Active participation in gaming forums and events may open up viable job opportunities and help a skilled and avid gamer build vital industry contacts. Any time invested in learning how to code and making your games may also prove beneficial in standing out among your peers in the job market.

Projected Career Map

Performance, experience, and the acquisition of professional qualifications drive the career progression of Game Testers who take on managerial and leadership roles with increased responsibilities. Most assume the role of a QA (Quality Assurance)Tester before they become QA Analysts.

>Six to seven years of dedicated work may help you advance to a Senior QA Analyst or QA Team Leader position. Specialist roles such as Developer and Designer can also form part of your career advancement plans once you acquire relevant skills and qualifications.

Beneficial Professional Development

Continuing professional development (CPD) will help an active Game Tester build personal skills and proficiency through work-based learning, a professional activity, formal education, or self-directed learning. It allows you to upskill continually, regardless of your age, job, or level of knowledge.

Attend short courses to stay updated on the latest gaming technology and software packages. Learn about the evolving market and adapt your programming and 3D modelling skills to help develop your career as a niche Game Tester.


Conclusion

With a whopping 40 per cent of the world devoted to playing video games, it is clear that Games Testers play a critical role in developing praise-worthy games. Their dedication to ironing out all sorts of bugs in an emerging game through countless gaming sessions makes them a vital asset to the gaming industry.

Advice from the Wise

Game Tester is a career path that will test your endurance with repetitive game testing sessions, so you need to enjoy gaming.
